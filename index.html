Designing a Career
While my academic background is in biomedical engineering, my true passion lies in design engineering. Throughout my education and career, I have gravitated toward the creative process of solving complex problems through innovative design. From developing functional prototypes to optimizing products for manufacturability, I have continually sought opportunities to refine my skills as a design engineer. My experiences have allowed me to step beyond the biomedical field and focus on the principles of product design, precision, and functionality that define exceptional engineering. Today, I see myself not as a medical devices engineer, but as a design engineer committed to creating impactful, efficient, and user-focused solutions.
I earned a Master of Science in Engineering from Johns Hopkins University in May 2023, where I honed my skills in product design and development. During my graduate studies, I worked on creating an efficient, user-centered incontinence management system for geriatric patients. This project involved identifying and validating user needs, designing solutions that balanced functionality and manufacturability, and transitioning concepts into prototypes ready for testing.
I also contributed to ARISE (Accelerated, Realistic, Intelligent Simulation for Surgical Education), where I played a key role in designing and refining a suite of surgical education tools. These products were developed with an emphasis on scalability, durability, and cost-effectiveness to support laparoscopic training in low- and middle-income countries. My work required collaboration across teams and disciplines, demonstrating my ability to create practical, impactful designs that address real-world challenges.
As an undergraduate, I worked in a biomaterials lab exploring solutions for cerebral aneurysm occlusion.
